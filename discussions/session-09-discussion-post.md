# Session 9 — Discussion Post

**Course:** ED452C  
**Theme:** Evidence of Inclusion — Access, Participation, Growth, and Belonging

---

Wehmeyer and Kurth’s reading made me think about evidence as more than proof that a student finished the task. If inclusion is working, the evidence should show access, participation, growth, and belonging, not just physical placement in the general education classroom. A student can be present in an inclusive setting and still be excluded if the only evidence we value is fast, conventional, or easy for adults to measure. This pushed me to think about my teaching placement differently. Some of the clearest evidence of learning did not appear at the cleanest moments of the lesson. It appeared after a student talked through an idea with a peer, revised a design, asked for the directions in another way, or entered the task later than others but with a stronger understanding than their first response showed. For students whose processing, language, communication, or sensory needs do not match the classroom’s default pace, evidence has to be collected across the learning process, not only at the first visible product.

For my broader puzzle plan and future teaching, this reading reinforces that inclusive education must be evaluated through multiple forms of evidence. Inclusion is not working just because students are in the room. It is working when the environment helps students show what they know, participate with dignity, and remain connected to the learning community.

My question is: How can teachers collect evidence of inclusion that captures belonging, participation, and student growth without reducing students to only test scores, task completion, or compliance?
