# Session 11 — Discussion Post

**Course:** ED452C  
**Theme:** SEL and PBIS as Relational and Structural Support

---

Exploring CASEL and PBIS made me think about student support as both relational and structural. CASEL focuses on social and emotional learning through self-awareness, self-management, social awareness, relationship skills, and responsible decision-making, while PBIS gives schools a tiered framework for building positive, predictable, equitable environments. Together, they show that inclusion is not only about responding to problems, but about designing routines and relationships that prevent students from being misunderstood in the first place. I find these to be very important for neurodivergent, culturally and linguistically diverse, disabled, multilingual, and multiply-identified students. I have seen how behavior and participation can be misread when schools use only one definition of “appropriate.” A student who needs processing time, communicates differently, or comes from a different cultural or linguistic background may be showing competence in ways that are easy to miss. Similarly, in my teaching placement, I have seen how routines, partner structures, clarification, and positive feedback shaped whether students felt safe enough to participate.

For my broader puzzle plan and future teaching, CASEL and PBIS reinforce that inclusive classrooms need both care and systems. Relationships matter, but they need to be supported by consistent routines, data-informed decisions, and culturally responsive expectations.

My question is: How can teachers use SEL and PBIS together so that support feels proactive and identity-centered, and not another way to monitor/correct students who already experience school differently?
