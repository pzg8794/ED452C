# Session 5 — Discussion Post

**Course:** ED452C  
**Theme:** Self-Determination as Causal Agency — Designing for Student Agency

---

Wehmeyer and Shogren’s reading made me reflect about self-determination as something students practice through meaningful participation, not something adults simply teach from the outside. If students are expected to become self-advocates, goal-setters, and decision-makers, then classrooms need to give them real opportunities to choose, explain, revise, and lead.

Personally, I believe this matters for neurodivergent, culturally and linguistically diverse, disabled, multilingual, and multiply-identified students because support can sometimes become over-direction. In my teaching placement, I have seen how small moments of agency mattered: students choosing a project direction, asking for clarification, explaining what helped them participate, or adjusting an idea after feedback. Those moments were not separate from learning; they were part of how students began to see themselves as capable contributors.

For my broader puzzle plan and future teaching, this reading reinforces that inclusive classrooms should not only remove barriers, but also create repeated opportunities for students to act as causal agents in their own learning.

My question is: How can teachers support students without taking over the decision-making opportunities they need in order to develop real self-determination?
