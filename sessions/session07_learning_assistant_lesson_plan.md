# ED452C Learning Assistant Lesson

**Session:** 7 - Evidence in Support of Inclusive Education  
**Date:** Tuesday, June 9  
**Format:** Short LaTeX/Beamer presentation with two discussion activities  
**Theme:** Evidence of inclusion as access, participation, growth, and belonging

## Lesson purpose

This lesson helps classmates think about evidence in inclusive education as more than physical placement, task completion, or quick performance. The central idea is that if inclusion is working, teachers should be able to see evidence of access, participation, growth, and belonging across the learning process.

## Flow

The lesson begins by framing evidence as a design question: what do our classrooms allow us to notice? It then asks classmates to consider how students can be present in inclusive classrooms but still excluded by narrow evidence practices. The middle of the lesson introduces multiple forms of evidence, including written products, peer explanation, teacher conferences, revision, questions students ask, participation, and confidence over time. The equity lens focuses on how neurodivergent, culturally and linguistically diverse, disabled, multilingual, and multiply identified students can be misread when evidence is limited to speed, compliance, or conventional formats.

## Activities

**Activity 1: Evidence Sort.** Students sort examples into easy-to-measure evidence, process evidence, and evidence we often miss. The discussion question is: Which students are most likely to be misread if we only use the first group?

**Activity 2: Redesign the Evidence Plan.** Students choose one lesson they teach or remember and add two process checkpoints, one student voice prompt, and one alternative way to show understanding. The guiding question is: What would you see now that you might have missed before?

## Closing teacher takeaway

Before concluding that a student did not learn, teachers should ask whether the student had access to the task, whether evidence was collected across the process, and whether the evidence plan valued belonging and participation. The final message is simple: look again before you conclude.
